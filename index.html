Executive Overview

This report presents findings from Technical Terminator LLC's cyber insurance readiness assessment of Acme Dental Associates ("Client"), a 4-location dental practice in the Dallas–Fort Worth area with approximately 42 employees. The assessment evaluated the Client's current cybersecurity posture against common underwriter benchmarks used by top-tier cyber insurers, including requirements aligned to the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF 2.0) and CIS Controls v8.

The assessment was conducted via intake questionnaire, a structured 60-minute discovery call, and review of three existing policy documents provided by the Client. No penetration testing or technical scanning was performed as part of this engagement.

Overall Readiness Score: 47 / 100 — The Client demonstrates several foundational security practices but has material gaps in documentation, access control, and incident preparedness that create meaningful risk of claim denial or premium surcharge.

Priority Action Plan

  2. 02.Document and execute a backup restoration test. Record the date, scope, and result. Retain this record for at least 12 months.

Incident Response Plan
CLIENT: Meridian Legal Group · Prepared by: Technical Terminator LLC
Version 1.2
Effective: January 2026
Review Date: January 2027
Purpose & Scope

This Incident Response Plan ("IRP") establishes the procedures Meridian Legal Group ("the Firm") will follow in the event of an actual or suspected cybersecurity incident. This plan applies to all Firm personnel, contractors, and third-party vendors with access to Firm systems or client data.

This document is designed to satisfy incident response documentation requirements of leading cyber insurance carriers and aligns with NIST SP 800-61r2 (Computer Security Incident Handling Guide) and applicable state bar cybersecurity guidance.

Incident Response Team
RoleResponsibilityPrimary Contact
Incident Response LeadOverall coordination; insurer notification[Managing Partner]
IT Point of ContactTechnical containment; evidence preservation[IT Manager / MSP]
Legal/ComplianceRegulatory notification obligations; client counsel[General Counsel]
Communications LeadInternal and external messaging[Office Manager]
Cyber Insurance CarrierCoverage activation; breach coach assignment[Carrier Contact / Policy #]
Response Phases
  1. Phase 1
    Identification — Any employee who suspects a security incident must immediately notify the IT Point of Contact and Incident Response Lead. Do not attempt to investigate independently or power off affected systems unless instructed. Log the time, observed symptoms, and affected systems. Incidents include (but are not limited to): ransomware messages, unauthorized access alerts, data exfiltration notifications, phishing that resulted in credential entry, or system anomalies.
  2. Phase 2
    Containment — The IT Point of Contact will isolate affected systems from the network (logical or physical) within 4 hours of incident confirmation. Do not wipe or reimage any systems until forensic evidence is captured. Preserve logs. Disable compromised accounts immediately.
  3. Phase 3
    Notification — The Incident Response Lead will contact the cyber insurance carrier within 24 hours of incident confirmation using the policy's breach reporting hotline. The carrier will assign a breach coach. Legal/Compliance will evaluate state breach notification obligations (Texas Business & Commerce Code §521.053 requires notification within 60 days of breach discovery when applicable).
  4. Phase 4
    Eradication & Recovery — Once containment is confirmed and forensic imaging complete, affected systems may be restored from clean backups. All restored systems must be verified clean before reconnection. Compromised credentials must be reset; MFA re-enrollment required for all affected accounts.
  5. Phase 5
    Post-Incident Review — Within 30 days of incident closure, the IRT will conduct a lessons-learned review. Findings will be documented and this plan updated as needed. A summary will be provided to the cyber insurance carrier upon request.
Critical: Notifying your cyber insurance carrier promptly — before engaging outside vendors or public relations — is one of the most common requirements for coverage activation. Failure to notify within the policy window may jeopardize your claim.
Plan Testing & Review

This IRP will be reviewed annually and following any declared incident. A tabletop exercise simulating a ransomware scenario should be conducted at minimum once per year. Exercise completion must be documented and retained.

Acceptable Use Policy
CLIENT: Summit Ridge Construction · Prepared by: Technical Terminator LLC
Version 1.0
Effective: February 2026
Review Date: February 2027
Purpose

Summit Ridge Construction ("the Company") provides employees, contractors, and authorized third parties with access to information technology resources to support business operations. This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") defines the standards for appropriate use of those resources and the responsibilities of all users.

Compliance with this policy supports the Company's cyber insurance obligations, protects against data loss and liability, and ensures the confidentiality of client and business information.

Scope

This policy applies to all employees, temporary staff, contractors, and vendors who access Company systems, networks, devices, or data — whether on Company premises or remotely. Covered resources include: company-owned and personal devices used for work (BYOD), email accounts, cloud services, project management platforms, and any system containing Company or client data.

Acceptable Use Standards
  • Use Company systems primarily for legitimate business purposes. Incidental personal use is permitted provided it does not consume excessive resources or expose the Company to risk.
  • Protect login credentials. Never share passwords or allow another individual to use your account. Use a unique, strong password for each system and enable multi-factor authentication where available.
  • Report suspicious emails, links, or attachments to IT before clicking. When in doubt, do not open.
  • Store work files in Company-approved platforms (e.g., SharePoint, OneDrive) rather than personal cloud storage or local drives.
  • Lock or log out of your workstation when leaving your desk, even briefly.
  • Do not install unauthorized software on Company devices without IT approval.
Prohibited Activities

The following are strictly prohibited and may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination:

  • Accessing, transmitting, or storing material that is illegal, discriminatory, or violates Company policies
  • Attempting to access systems, accounts, or data beyond your authorized level
  • Disabling or circumventing security software, VPNs, or monitoring tools
  • Using Company resources for personal commercial activity or outside business ventures
  • Transmitting client or proprietary data to unauthorized personal accounts or devices
  • Connecting unsecured personal devices to the Company network without IT approval
Users should have no expectation of privacy when using Company-owned systems or networks. The Company reserves the right to monitor, access, and review activity on these resources.
Acknowledgment

All employees and contractors must sign an acknowledgment form confirming receipt and understanding of this policy upon onboarding and annually thereafter. Signed acknowledgments are retained in personnel records and constitute documentation of policy awareness for cyber insurance and regulatory purposes.
